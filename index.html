Nissan GT-R

Overview

The Nissan GT-R, also known as the R35, is a high-performance sports car and grand tourer produced by Nissan since 2007. It features a 2+2 seating layout and is renowned for its advanced technology and impressive performance.

Specifications

  • Engine: 3.8L twin-turbocharged VR38DETT V6
  • Power Output: Up to 592 hp (Nismo version)
  • Transmission: 6-speed dual-clutch
  • Drivetrain: All-wheel drive
  • Top Speed: Approximately 196 mph (315 km/h)
  • 0-60 mph: Around 2.9 seconds

Performance

The GT-R is known for its exceptional performance, thanks to its powerful engine, advanced all-wheel-drive system, and sophisticated aerodynamics. It has set numerous records on various racetracks around the world.

Design

The design of the GT-R is both functional and stylish, with a focus on aerodynamics and high-speed stability. It features a distinctive front grille, aggressive lines, and a wide stance.

History

The GT-R name has a rich history, originating from the Nissan Skyline GT-R. The R35 GT-R, however, is a standalone model that continues the legacy of high performance and innovation.

Recent Updates

The 2025 Nissan GT-R includes special editions and enhancements to honor its legacy. It continues to be a benchmark in the sports car segment with its blend of power, technology, and craftsmanship.
